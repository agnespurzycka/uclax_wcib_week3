PANNA COTTA WITH BERRIES

  • Level: Easy
  • Total Time: 6h 12min
  • Prep: 5 min
  • Inactive: 6 hr
  • Cook: 7 min
  • Yield: 6 servings

INGREDIENTS:

  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1 tablespoon unflavored powdered gelatin
  • 3 cups whipping cream
  • 1/3 cup of honey
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • Pinch salt
  • 3 cups assorted fresh berries
  • 2 to 3 tablespoons of sugar
  • 1 tablespoon freshly squezzed lemon juice
  • Zest of 1 lemon

DIRECTIONS:

Place the milk in a small bowl. Sprinkle the gelatin over. Let stand for 3 to 5 minutes to soften the gelatin. Pour mixture into heavy saucepan and stir over medium heat just until the gelatin dissolves but the milk does not boil, about 5 minutes. Add the cream, honey, sugar, and salt. Stir until the sugar dissolves,5 to 7 minutes. Remove from the heat. Pour into 6 glasses so that they are half full. Cool slightly. Refrigerate, until set, at least 6 hours.

For the berry sauce: in a medium saucepot set over medium heat, add the berries, sugar, lemon juice and zest. Bring to a boil and cook until the berries burst and the liquid starts to thicken, about 5 minutes. Strain the mixture through a fine mesh sieve, forcing the berries through by pushing and scraping with a rubber spatula. The more you force through, the thicker the sauce willl be (the pupl closest to the seeds contains most pectin, which is a natural thickener). Let cool complitely and store in refrigerator until ready to serve. Spoon the sauce over the panna cotta and serve
